About KNU:

Founded in 1834, Kyiv National University (KNU) is Ukraine's oldest and most prestigious higher education institution. With a rich history spanning nearly two centuries, the university has maintained a tradition of academic excellence and modern innovation. KNU is renowned for its dedication to quality education and its role in shaping the future of Ukraine. Currently, KNU comprises 13 faculties, 7 educational and scientific institutes, 3 educational institutes, 1 educational and scientific center, 2 colleges, 1 lyceum, and preparatory departments. The university boasts a diverse and dynamic student body, with more than 25,000 students, including about 2,000 international students, pursuing over 600 academic programs in 25 fields and 59 disciplines. Kyiv National University consistently ranks as one of the top universities in Eastern Europe and is highly regarded worldwide. Its graduates include prominent scientists, statesmen, and even two former presidents of Ukraine.
